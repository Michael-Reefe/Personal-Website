About me

I am currently a first-year physics graduate student and NSF GRFP fellow at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology working with Professor Mike McDonald. I'm primarily observationally and data-oriented and am currently focused on integral field spectroscopy. I came from George Mason University, where I earned my Bachelor's of Science in physics with a concentration in astrophysics in 2022.

During my undergraduate studies, I worked on exoplanet transits and radial velocities (RVs), and active galactic nuclei (AGN) interactions with their host galaxies. More specifically, I worked on projects automating the GMU telescope in Python; performing RV validations of the planets AU Mic b, c, and TOI 620 b; and searching for coronal emission lines in SDSS spectra. Some common threads that are shared between these interests are the analysis of high-resolution spectra and coding. Lots of coding. Luckily for me, I love coding, and sometimes even do it in my free time.

Some other hobbies of mine (besides coding) include hiking, video games, and dungeons & dragons.
